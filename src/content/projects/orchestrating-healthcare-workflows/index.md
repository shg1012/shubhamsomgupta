## Summary

The work explored how care teams could coordinate high-volume clinical operations without losing context between people, systems, and decisions.

## Role and responsibilities

- Led workflow discovery and translated operational findings into product principles.
- Designed the interaction model for queues, timeline states, and high-risk handoffs.
- Built a reusable component language for dense clinical information surfaces.

## Problem or opportunity

Teams were switching between disconnected sources of truth, which made prioritisation and escalation feel reactive rather than deliberate.

## Design principles

- Make status visible before requiring investigation.
- Preserve context when moving between patient, task, and team views.
- Create calm density instead of hiding operational complexity.

## Final solution

The proposed experience combined queue health, patient context, workflow timelines, and embedded decision support into one connected command surface.

## Reflection

The most valuable design move was treating workflow transparency as a care quality feature, not only an operational efficiency feature.
